Why an Accessible Routing Options for George Mason University?

As GMU already complies with the set standards and protocols for accessibility, Why do we need an application to provide accessible routing options?

  • Lack of Real time accessible route options

    With fast-paced constructions, autonomous robot halts as well as some transient obstacles present in the pathway, accessible paths may be blocked. And this might cause issues during navigation for people with mobility impairments.

  • Lack of Obstacle Reporting Mechanism

    Even when an obstacle is detected, there seem to be lack of obstacle reporting. Reporting could help future commuters plan their new route and reach to destination on time.

Problems faced by People with mobility impairments

  • Case Study

    George Mason University

    For our Case Study, we chose a path around Student Union Building-1 on George Mason University Fairfax campus. This area was chosen primarily because:

    • - Office of Disability Services is located within Student Union Building-1
    • - Heavy constructions and obstructions around this area.

    There is an Accessibility map provided by the campus. But:

    • - The map is outdated. It was last updated on June 12, 2019 so there is no current update of route accessibility.
    • - Newer buildings like Horizon Hall are not included in the map.
